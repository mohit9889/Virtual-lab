Theory of Machines

Four link planar mechanisms are the simplest of all mechanisms. A large variety of motions is possible even with only four links with different combinations of kinematic pairs and with different inversions of chain.

In a four bar linkage the link not connected to frame is called coupler whereas two links hinged to the frame are called crank and follower. Normally, the driving member is referred as the crank and other as the follower. Also, out of these two, the link which can rotate completely in a circle is termed as crank and the one which cannot complete a circle about its fixed joint, is called as rocker.
